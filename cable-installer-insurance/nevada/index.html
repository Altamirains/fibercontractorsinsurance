FAQ — Nevada

Nevada Cable Installer Insurance Questions

Common questions from Las Vegas and Nevada cable TV installers, CATV contractors, and coax technicians about insurance requirements.

Do cable installers in Nevada need general liability insurance?

Yes. Nevada cable installers working for cable TV providers and ISPs are required to carry general liability insurance. Nevada's NSCB licensing requirements and most MSO subcontractor agreements require proof of GL before any cable installation work begins. Resort and casino GCs typically require higher limits than standard residential or commercial work.

Is workers' compensation required for cable installers in Nevada?

Yes. Nevada requires all employers with one or more employees to carry workers' compensation insurance. This includes cable TV installers, coax contractors, and low voltage cable technicians operating anywhere in Nevada. Nevada's workers' comp requirements are strictly enforced and non-compliance can result in significant fines.

How much does cable installer insurance cost in Nevada?

Cable installer insurance in Nevada typically starts around $850–$2,100/year for general liability depending on your revenue, trade type, and operations. Casino and resort work may require higher limits. Submit a quote request above for accurate Nevada pricing from multiple A-rated carriers.

What insurance do cable subcontractors need for Las Vegas casino and resort jobs?

Las Vegas casino and resort cable installation projects typically require higher liability limits — often $2M–$5M general liability and a $5M–$10M umbrella. Casino owners and resort GCs require comprehensive COIs naming them as additional insured before permitting any cable work on their properties. We specialize in meeting Nevada gaming and hospitality insurance requirements.

Do MDU cable installers in Nevada need their own insurance?

Yes. MDU cable installers working in Nevada apartment complexes, condos, and commercial buildings must carry their own general liability and workers' comp. Las Vegas and Henderson property managers, HOAs, and building owners require COIs before allowing cable installation work on premises.
