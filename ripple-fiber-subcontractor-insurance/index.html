Ripple Fiber Subcontractor Insurance Questions

Common questions from Georgia fiber contractors getting insured for Ripple Fiber subcontract work.

What insurance do I need to work as a Ripple Fiber subcontractor?

Ripple Fiber typically requires: General Liability ($1M/$2M), statutory Workers' Compensation, Commercial Auto ($1M CSL), and possibly an Umbrella policy for larger projects. Ripple Fiber must be named as Additional Insured on your GL and Auto policies. We issue compliant COIs promptly.

Does Georgia require Workers' Comp for Ripple Fiber subcontractors?

Georgia state law requires Workers' Compensation for employers with 3 or more employees. However, Ripple Fiber's subcontract agreements typically require Workers' Comp regardless of crew size. We strongly advise all Ripple Fiber subcontractors — even sole proprietors — to carry Workers' Comp to meet Ripple's subcontract requirements.

How fast can I get a Ripple Fiber-compliant COI?

We issue prompt COIs for Ripple Fiber subcontractors. Once your coverage is bound, we can issue a COI naming Ripple Fiber as Additional Insured the same business day — so you never miss a Ripple Fiber contract over paperwork delays.
